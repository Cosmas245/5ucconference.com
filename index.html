CONFERENCE ON: WATER AND POPULATION HEALTH (WAPO)

Summary

On the 11th of November 2023 the Vice Chancellors (VCs) from five universities: University of Dar Es Salaam (UDSM), Sokoine University of Agriculture (SUA), Nelson Mandela African Institution of Science and Technology (NM-AIST), Aga Khan University (AKU) and Simon Fraser University (SFU) met at the AKU’s Arusha Campus. After their deliberation, the five universities VCs agreed, as a starting point to invest resources on water and health as a uniting and priority theme to kickstart the consortium’s collaborative research work. The second theme is data analytics which will be based on the analysis of large data to be gathered on the priority theme.

Background and rationale

Around the globe, current and anticipated effects of climate change and land use changes are posing significant threats on water and population health. Climate change does not follow any borders and developing countries are bearing the worst of the issue despite their lack of contribution (Lenton et al., 2023). Climate change further disadvantages higher populations, or growing populations in hot places as the proportion of people exposed to harm from higher temperatures is greater (Lenton et al., 2023). Tanzania and the surrounding region are vulnerable to climate hazards and therefore need to be prepared and equipped to adapt and cope with the coming changes (Atwoli et al., 2022; Gaborit, 2022a). Tanzania is experiencing worsening droughts, flash floods from torrential rainfalls, and declining surface and underground water volumes and quality. Yet, the impact of climate change on human health is insufficiently studied, and work is needed to understand and prioritize highly needed investments in basic infrastructure and water policies and governance to help mitigate these problems in the country. The proposed closed conference by the five universities consortium will leverage the existing expertise within each university to work on these priority themes.

Conference Theme: Water, Health and Data Analytics in a Changing Climate

Topics for presentation and discussion may include those below and related and interdisciplinary topics that combine two or more of the areas below.  We particularly encourage presentations that cross topics among those of climate change, water, health and large data sets, and are relevant to especially East Africa.

Water

Understanding various dimensions of water security in East Africa – safe drinking water, water for agriculture and industry, water-energy-food nexus, and integrated water resource management (IWRM).  Factors influencing water quality; access to water, sanitation and hygiene (WaSH) services; ways to ameliorate water scarcity and improve water access and water management systems (e.g. resilient water systems), water conservation land practices, riparian health, forests as natural water regulators, water pollution in the African Great Lakes basin and potential improvements/solutionswater and human migration, herders and their adaptive understand of water access, water and gender relationships, water and  opportunities.

Health:

Advances in the One Health concept and its applications in East Africa. Pathogens resistance as a result of climate change. Recent E. African trends and relationships associated with nutrition/ malnutrition, infectious disease (e.g. HIV/AIDs, tuberculosis), vector-borne diseases (e.g., malaria, dengue), reproductive and maternal health, and children’s pneumonia, water-borne diseases, measles, and other infectious diseases.  Access to high quality medicines; approaches to avoid and address pandemics; trends of and ways to address non-communicable diseases.

Data Analytics:

Data analytics means  the use of ‘Big data’ and machine-learning tools that allow analysis of  large data sets, often from multiple sources  to identify trends or patterns.  Some possible topics under this umbrella include enhancing data mobility around water/rainfall data, hydrology, crop yields, meteorology, agronomy, fisheries and livelihoods.  How can big data analytics invite analyses that will help the end-users understand impacts of climate  change  on the communities in East Africa? Using data analytics to inform environmental planning, climate change adaptation planning, business and economic development in E. Africa. 

Structure of the talk / presentation:

The main purpose of the consortium is to create an opportunity for scholars from the member universities to meet, interact with one another, learn from each other’s work and share their research priorities and methods.  Presenters should also mention the regions in which they have worked.  This invite-only conference is an opportunity for scholars to explore research collaboration, work toward partnerships, and consider faculty exchange across the five institutions. This conference, therefore, does not follow the traditional academic conference presentation program.  We encourage presenters to talk at a general level so that interdisciplinary cross-fertilization of research ideas becomes more likely. The presentation should focus on what general questions (avoid too much jargon) you ask with your research, describe the research methods you tend to use (avoid too much detail), and speak to what policy, problem, or broader issue your research can inform or generally informs.  We suggest you provide sufficient big picture background (and pictures are welcome in your presentation) so that everyone can follow where your work fits both in terms of the issues in the place in which you do the research, and the issues for the scholarly area in which you work.  Your presentation might follow the kind of explanations that are delivered in Ted Talks.  Your talk should be 15 minutes long.  A general rule of thumb is 2 minutes per slide so your talk should be 7-8 slides long.  Please provide your contact information on the last slide.
